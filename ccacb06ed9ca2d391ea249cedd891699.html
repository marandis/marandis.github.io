3. Nettle root.

With age, an increasing amount of male hormone binds to globulin, and therefore less the male hormone reaches cellular receptors, weakening the male sexual organ. Therefore, it is necessary to increase the level of this so-called "free male hormone" to enhance the male sexual organ. The hormone that controls the level of "free male hormone" is called SHBG (the hormone that binds sexual hormones hormones). male hormone binds to globulin and becomes a "bound male hormone", and the body needs a "free male hormone". When men turn 45, the hormone SHBG dramatically increases its effect, and this, of course, means the loss of the male sexual organ. In fact, with age, a decrease in sexual desire this is not because of the amount of male hormone produced, but because the binding of male hormone to globulin increases.

If you take an artificial male hormone, it is re-bound by the hormone SHBG. Therefore, men who undergo such therapy do not have a male sexual organ organ-effect. Logically, the binding of male hormone to globulin should be blocked. Thus, the body will have more "free male hormone", which is actually responsible for the male sexual organ. Here, nettle root extract comes to the rescue. Its high concentration provides a unique mechanism of increasing the level of "free" male hormone. Components of nettle roots bind to globulin instead of male hormone and thus reduce the binding of "free" male hormone, which leads to an increase in sexual desire.

D-aspartic acid is formed when the enzyme aspartatracemase converts L-aspartic acid to D-aspartic acid. This amino acid plays a key role in the production of sperm and sex hormones.
